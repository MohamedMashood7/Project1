40,000 MT of Petrol to reach SL tonight

The Ministry of Power and Energy said that a vessel carrying 40,000 MT of petrol is scheduled to reach the country today (19). The Ministry said that the payments have been made for the relevant vessel. Also, the Ministry of Power and Energy says that over 100,000 people have registered for the National Fuel Pass, and the distribution of new fuel stocks will NOT take place until the registration is completed. In the meantime, many tense situations were reported from island-wide filling stations yesterday (18). A heated situation had taken place at the Mahinyanganaya Lanka IOC filling station yesterday (18). Police Spokesman SSP Nihal Thalduwa said that the Police has fired in the air to control the situation.

Saudi Aramco posts biggest quarterly profit

Saudi Aramco posted the biggest quarterly adjusted profit of any listed company globally driven by high crude prices and production. Aramco followed big oil rivals by reporting a surge in profits. Net income rose to $48.4 billion in the second quarter, up from $25.5 billion a year earlier, the state-controlled company said on Sunday. Its free cash flow rose by 53% from a year earlier to $34.6 billion. The company is using the windfall to reduce debt and invest in a huge expansion of its production capacity.
